# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Windows users who want their calendar and lightweight tasks to stay visible on the desktop while they work. The early target includes independent developers, students, remote workers, and project managers who find full calendar apps too heavy for quick planning.

## Product Purpose

Dayboard is a local-first desktop calendar and task board. It should make the next few days of work visible at a glance, support direct task creation and editing from the calendar surface, and stay quiet enough to live on the desktop for long sessions.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, efficient, grounded. Dayboard should feel like a calm Windows productivity surface, not a branded marketing app or a decorative widget.

## Anti-references

Avoid heavy calendar suites, busy toolbars, marketing-style landing pages, always-visible product branding, oversized headers, and small novelty widgets that cannot show useful task detail. Avoid treating Gmail or Outlook sync as the MVP's main story before the local desktop workflow is stable.

## Design Principles

- Calendar first: the main surface should reserve most space for the date grid and visible task titles.
- Edit in place: empty date space creates tasks, existing items open editing, and common changes stay on the board.
- Quiet desktop presence: controls, branding, and account status should remain low-emphasis until needed.
- User-controlled system behavior: opacity, fixed-window mode, rail side, and startup behavior must be explicit settings.
- Local-first trust: no sync or sensitive account handling should be assumed before the secure storage path is designed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for readable contrast on translucent surfaces, keyboard-reachable controls, clear focus states, and reduced-motion-friendly interactions. Since this is a desktop widget, preserve legibility at half-screen and narrow pinned sizes before adding visual effects.
